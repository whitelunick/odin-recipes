Omurice

An easy Japanese style omelette filled with fried rice

Omurice is a popular Japanese and Western fusion dish that combines an omelette with Japanese fried rice. The rice is usally pan-fried with ketchup and chicke, then wrapped in a thin sheet of egg omlette. It's a quick and easy comfort food!

Ingredients:

For Ketchup Fried Rice:

  • 1/2 onion
  • 1 boneless, skinless chicken thighs
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1/2 cup frozen mixed vegetables
  • 1/8 tsp salt
  • 1/8 tsp black pepper
  • 2 servings cooked Japanese short-grain rice
  • 1 tbsp ketchup
  • 1 tsp soy sauce

For Omelette:

  • 2 large eggs (seperated)
  • 2 tbsp milk (seperated)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil (seperated)
  • 6 tbsp shreddded sharp cheddar cheese

Cooking Instructions:

For Ketchup Fried Rice:

  1. Chop the onion finely
  2. Cut the chicke into 1/2" (1.3 cm) pieces
  3. Heat the oil in a non-stick pan and saut√© the onion until softened
  4. Add the chicken and cook until no longer pink
  5. Add the mixed vegetables and season with salt and pepper
  6. Add the rice and break into small pieces
  7. Add ketchup and soy sauce and combine everything evenly with a spatula. Transfer the fried rice to a plate and wash the pan.

For Omelette

  1. We'll make one omurice at a time. Whisk 1 egg and 1 tbsp milk in a small bowl
  2. Heat 1 tbsp olive oil in the pan over medium high heat
  3. When the pan is hot, pour egg mixture into the pan. Titled to cover the bottom of the pan. Lower the heat when the bottom of the egg has set
  4. Put the tbsp cheese and half of the ketchup fried rice on top of the omelette.
  5. Use the spatula to fold both sides of omelette toward the middle to cover the fried rice. Slowly move the omurice to the edge of the pan.
  6. Hold a plate in one hand and the pan in the other hand, flip the pan and move the omurice to the plate.
  7. While it's still hot, cover the omurice with a paper towel and shape it into a football shape. Repeat the process to make a second omurice.
