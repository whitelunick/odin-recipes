Tamagoyaki

A rolled omelette makes for a bento staple

Tamagoyaki is a sweetened Japanese rolled omelette with a custardy texture. It's well-loved amongst children and adults alike, and you'll find it as a staple in Japanese breakfast and bento!

Ingredients:

  • 3 large eggs
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil

For Seasoning:

  • 3 tbsp dashi
  • 2 tsp sugar
  • 1 tsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp mirin
  • 2 pinch salt

Cooking Instructions:

  1. Gently whisk eggs in a bowl. Do not over mix.
  2. In another bowl combine seasoning ingredients and mix well.
  3. Pour the seasoning mixture into the egg mixture and whisk gently. Then pour the mixture into a measure cup with a spout.
  4. Heat a tamagoyaki pan over medium heat, dip a paper towel in oil and apply to the pan. Check the heat with a little bit of egg.
  5. When you hear the sizzling sound, pour a thin layer of egg mixture in the pan, tilting to cover the bottom of the pan.
  6. Poke the air ubbles. After the bottom of the egg has set, but still soft on top, start rolling into a log shape from one side to the other.
  7. Move the rolled omelette to the side where you started to roll. Apply more oil with a paper towel.
  8. Pour the egg mixture to cover the bottom of the pan again. Even under the already rolled omelette.
  9. When the new layer of egg has set and still soft on top, start rolling from one side to the other.
  10. Repeat method a 3rd, 4th, 5th, and 6th time, making sure to poke air bubbles.
  11. Remove from the pan and place on a bamboo sushi mat and wrap it up. Shape the egg while it's still hot. Let it stand for 5 minutes.
  12. Remove the omelette from the sushi mat. Cut into 1/2" (1 cm) pieces.
